Fossil Facts

Long before humans walked the Earth, dinosaurs roamed the planet for millions of years. Today, we learn about these ancient creatures through their fossilized remainsâ€”mostly bones that have turned to stone over time.

Fossils form when a dinosaur dies and gets buried by mud, sand, or other sediment. Over thousands or even millions of years, minerals slowly replace the bone material, creating a rock-like replica of the original skeleton. These natural records give us a glimpse into life during the Mesozoic Era.

Paleontologists study these fossils to understand what dinosaurs looked like, how they moved, what they ate, and how they lived. From tiny toe bones to giant skulls, every fossil discovery adds a piece to the puzzle of Earth's prehistoric past.

Without these bones, dinosaurs would be a complete mystery. Thanks to fossils, we know that creatures like the Tyrannosaurus rex had sharp teeth for hunting, while dinosaurs like Brachiosaurus had long necks to reach high vegetation. Fossils truly bring history back to life!
